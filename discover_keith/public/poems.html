Big brown box

by Keithacrawford on May 15, 2017.  ©
A gift on my doorstep
eyes squinting
mouth wide open

Tearing down the gift wrap
I find a big, brown box
Is there gold underneath all this wrap?

I open the covers of the big, brown box
Another slightly smaller, brown box
Forehead lines stress

Opening the covers again
What a surprise
Another big, brown box

As my brain gears chug
I open the top
revealing a rainbow colored box

The tape is like super glue
the rainbow box resists like metal
Scissors, knives fail to cut

My hands drained
I toss it in the closet
Weeks pass by as my life flashes forward
Before I know it I'm out of school
and looking to fill the hole in my heart

Yet each date is a brown box
Big, brown, hallow boxes
A sight for the eyes
but poison to the mind

Finally when all hope is lost
I meet one who glows
Not because of her look
nor because of her brain
but the look in her eyes is a rainbow

As we talk I find sands of time flow faster
My laugh reaches the sky
My smile beams wider
My confidence turns from bronze to gold

However, joining our rivers
My words are lost in the dark
I can't crack her code

Finally, one day as I wake up
I walk to my closet to get my jacket
As I turn on the lights in the closet
I glance at an open rainbow box

I head over to check it out
my face in a daze at the maze being solved
I look down into the box
There is a picture with words lined up

The picture is the women I've been seeing
Her smile so sweet and brilliant as an unpolluted night sky
The words read
"Love is a simple thing, but people make it complex"

Looking back on those words
The big brown boxes came apart like paper
The rainbow box tight like a fortress wall

My next date
The ring in my pocket
I stepped up to her
with the boxes in mind
my lips moved on their own
My eyes opened with sweat on my forehead





Author notes

I'm not even sure if I can call this a poem, it was more like a story that I condensed into a poem. My inspiration for it is how the person you love can be anyone and you can find them at any point in your life. You can fuss over what to say to them all you want but in the end it's best to be yourself and keep things simple. You should never put on a show with the one you love. This story poem is a message to myself and my own troubles but if it helps anyone else that reads it I will be happy.

A poet's mind

by Keithacrawford on April 11, 2016.  ©
To paint a picture
But never painting the final touch
An inspiration that comes from the heart
And is put into words by the mind's summary
A poet does not need a reward
The satisfaction is in writing a clear message yet still a mystery
A non-mystery is not a poem
It is simply an essay with a clear point
The original message is lost in future experiences
But the words are entrenched in history
Always open to interpretation
A worthless poem can become worthwhile
A worthwhile poem can become worthless
For time is just another poem
A clear message yet still a mystery


The Human Mind

by Keithacrawford on November 14, 2015.  ©
It's an endless story
A constant struggle with reality
A stubbornness to deny ones weaknesses
And show off strengths
Thinking only of how to impress
Rather than removing the guilt that causes stress
A haunting collector of evil thoughts
The endless amount of locks
Hiding the primal wants of a human
The woman's want of a man
And the man's want of a woman
The anger collected over the years
Secretly the mind tears
Wanting something to make the pain go away
Causing one to think the irrational way
Confusing an innocent personality into a killer
The minds an instant thriller
An unsolvable maze
Driving curiosity to discover the secrets of the universe
The mind is the biggest secret in this verse
Driving us away from it
Yet it's the key to understanding every bit
Every little bit of our never-ending madness
Even the reason I'm writing this now
Can not be explained until the mind gives a final bow
To its never-ending contest against our souls
